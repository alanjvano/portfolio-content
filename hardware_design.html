The frame of the hexacopter must be durable, yet light as possible. Any unnecessary weight will put a greater strain on the motors resulting in a less thrust and shorter flight time. Although carbon-fiber frames would be most ideal for this they are exepensive are must be custom made. For this project, a simple piece of plywood was used as the center structure for the hexacopter. Each of the six arms were constructed from aluminium L-shaped bars. Alumninum was chosen as it is lightweight and the L-shape provided durability across two directions. Lego pieces where then used to contruct a battery compartment and served as mountings for all the electronics. Legos were used for this task as they are cheap, light, easily configurable and did not have an load-bearing purposes.

The following components were used:

  • Raspberry Pi 4
  • Playstation 2 Controller
  • Turnigy D3530/14 1100KV Brushless Outrunner Motor (x6)
  • Turnigy MultiStar 40A BLHeli-S Rev 16 ESC v3 (x6)
  • Turnigy 5000mAh 3S 20C LiPo Battery
  • SunFounder PCA9685 16 Channel 12 Bit PWM Controller
  • Power Distribution Board
  • LED Controller Board
  • HiLetgo MPU9250 GY-9250 Motion Processing Unit
  • JBtek BMP180 Barometric Pressure, Temperature and Altitude Sensor
  • BN-180 GPS Module
  • 3.3V/5V Switching Power Module

The construction test of the hexacoper was accomplished primarily using threaded nuts and bolts for the connection attachments. Each of the six arms is bolted in place to the central wooden base. When possible the onboard electronics were also bolted into place. However certain components, such as the ESC motor controllers on each arm, were attached using zip ties and glue. If necessary, thread-lock can be used to ensure all the screw connections to not become loose.
